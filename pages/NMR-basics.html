NMR Basics

NMR relies on the magnetic moments of nuclei, μ, orienting themselves in different ways when an external magnetic field, B, is applied. The number of allowable orientations, nI, depends on the nuclear spin, I, according to equation 1.

Equation 1: nI = 2I + 1

These orientations are assigned magnetic quantum numbers, mI, with values ranging from +I to -I with a step size of 1. The energy, E, of these states is proportional to the nuclear moment and the applied field, according to equation 2. Where γ is the magnetogyric ratio of the nucleus, and h is the Planck constant.

Equation 2: E = - h mI B γ 1/2π

Example: Hydrogen

Property Symbol Value
Nuclear spin I 1/2
Magnetogyric Ratio γ 267.52

Hydrogen has a value of I equal to 1/2, and has 2 allowable orientations with magnetic quantum numbers mI = 1/2 and mI = -1/2.

Red protons are in low energy α state, and blue are in high energy β state. An α to β transition increases the total energy of the system and occurs when a photon is absorbed, and a β to α transition reduces the total energy of the system, emitting a photon.
